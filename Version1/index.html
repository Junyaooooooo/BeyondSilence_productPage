Many older adults struggle with loneliness, especially after retiring, losing a spouse, or facing health challenges that limit mobility. This isolation makes it harder for them to stay connected with their community, leading to emotional distress, a decline in mental and physical health, and a lower quality of life.

This RPG game is designed to shed light on the social isolation that often affects older adults. Through this game, the aim is to create a deeper understanding of these issues and encourage players to reflect on the importance of community and connection in later life.

The target audience for this RPG game includes young adults and middle-aged players who enjoy games with meaningful stories and social themes. It’s also aimed at people who care about family, community, and social issues, especially those who want to learn more about the challenges faced by older adults in a thoughtful, engaging way.

Storyboard

The story begins with the sound of a door closing. The game’s map should have two floors: the first floor contains the nurse’s office, the inpatient ward, and the elevator; the second floor includes the activity area and a fake bus stop for residents.
The game ends with a sudden event—a heavy rainstorm hits the town at night, marking the death of an elderly resident. The player attends the funeral, gaining a deeper understanding of the concept of “life and death.”

About Game

Mechanics

Exploration: Navigate the game and uncover key locations with clues.
Interaction: Unlock Ada’s memories by talking to NPCs and interacting with items.
Investigation: Ask questions, listen closely, and piece together the mystery of Ada's death.

Story

A high school girl travels through a time rift to a quiet town cared for by robots. She meets elderly residents, uncovers their memories, and learns about loneliness, loss, and the fleeting nature of time.
